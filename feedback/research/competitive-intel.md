### DataDriven.Golf
**URL:** https://datadriven.golf
**Methodology:** shot-by-shot (true Broadie strokes gained), calibrated to any handicap target
**Creator:** kinghippo33 (active on r/golf, beta testing)
**User-reported comparison:** kinghippo33 ran the same round through both tools and posted comparison screenshots. "Seems somewhat aligned but some differences in short game." Approach and short game (ATG) categories diverged most between the two tools. Putting was the area of closest agreement. The divergence is methodological: DataDriven tracks each shot against expected strokes from that position, while we compare scorecard stats (GIR%, scramble rate) against peer benchmarks.
**Strengths vs us:**
- More precise per-shot attribution
- Captures quality of misses (narrow GIR miss credited as good approach)
- True strokes gained methodology (Broadie)
- Better for players already tracking shot-by-shot data
**Weaknesses vs us:**
- Requires shot-by-shot input (higher friction)
- Not accessible to golfers who only have a scorecard
- Benchmarks against Tour-level expectations rather than peer handicap brackets
**Positioning insight:** We own the "scorecard + 5 minutes" lane. DataDriven owns the "shot tracker" lane. These are complementary, not competitive -- different tools for different input levels. Our growth comes from golfers who don't track shots, which is the vast majority.

### Tempo
**URL:** https://www.playtempo.golf/
**Category:** In-round shot-tracking app (iOS, coming soon as of March 2026)
**Methodology:** shot-by-shot, in-round logging with club-level tracking
**User-reported comparison:** No direct user comparison yet -- Tempo is pre-launch. Positioned as lightweight shot tracking: set up bag, tap each shot during round, review post-round stats (radar charts, heat maps, round comparisons against tour players).
**Strengths vs us:**
- Club-by-club stat breakdowns
- Visual insights (radar charts, heat maps) similar to our outputs
- Round-over-round comparison features
- Mobile-native workflow designed for on-course use
**Weaknesses vs us:**
- Requires phone interaction during the round (higher friction, behavior change)
- Benchmarks against tour players, not handicap peers
- iOS-only, pre-launch -- unproven adoption
- Still depends on manual shot logging despite "less phone, more golf" positioning
**Positioning insight:** Adjacent competitor, not direct. Tempo reinforces the core tradeoff: even apps that promise minimal phone use still require in-round interaction. This validates our thesis that many golfers prefer insight after the round rather than more phone usage during play. We stay in the "scorecard + 5 minutes post-round" lane; Tempo lives in the "shot tracker during play" lane. Monitor but don't react.
