# Personas

Emerging user archetypes from feedback patterns. Feeds `user-research-synthesis` and `feature-spec` (user stories).

---

### Scorecard Analyzer
**Handicap range:** 10-20
**Tools they use:** Scorecard apps (GHIN, 18Birdies), no shot trackers
**What they value:** Quick post-round insight without extra data entry; seeing where they stack up against similar players
**How they found us:** Reddit r/golf, word of mouth
**Key quotes:**
- Jiinker: "Nice work OP. If and when it warms up in Michigan enough to play i will be sure to use it."
- Beneficial_Present29: "Nice this is neat! I'll check it out later"
- BOYLANATOR: "The putting and around the green stats are interesting to me so I would use it for that."
**Feature priorities:** Keep input simple, shareable results, more handicap brackets

---

### Distance-Aware Player
**Handicap range:** 5-15
**Tools they use:** May use shot trackers (Arccos, Shot Scope) or at least knows their distances; possibly uses DataDriven.Golf
**What they value:** Precision in SG attribution; wants the tool to account for driving distance, approach distance, and shot quality
**How they found us:** Reddit r/golf, cross-referencing with other SG tools
**Key quotes:**
- BOYLANATOR: "Off the Tee strokes gained without tracking distance is pointless... There's no way they are gaining shots off the tee on me."
- BOYLANATOR: "I probably wouldn't want to enter estimated distances for every shot. So what you have is good."
- DontStalkMeNow (1.2 hcp): "The problem with it being transferred to another category is that you lose insight into which area of your game you need to improve and ultimately skewers your decision making."
- Candymanshook: "Allowing users to self-report approach distances? It's a lot easier than having them input drive distance."
- kinghippo33: Created DataDriven.Golf, posted side-by-side comparison screenshots
**Feature priorities:** Approach distance input (but must be optional/low-friction), more granular OTT breakdown, methodology transparency

### Underserved Golfer
**Handicap range:** 15-30+
**Tools they use:** Basic scorecard tracking; avoids Tour-based SG tools because the comparisons feel irrelevant
**What they value:** Being benchmarked against realistic peers rather than professionals; feeling included in golf analytics
**How they found us:** Reddit r/golf, possibly women's golf communities
**Key quotes:**
- LBGolfer: "As a female golfer seems your model may even be more useful than conventional SG, as the course rating/slope account for women's hitting distances. SG using male Tour data is less useful to me as it doesn't distinguish between skill and men's longer hitting distances."
**Feature priorities:** More handicap brackets (especially higher brackets), female-specific benchmarks, beginner-friendly explanations
