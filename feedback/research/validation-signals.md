# Validation Signals

Feeds `stakeholder-comms` and roadmap confidence scoring.

---

### Product-market fit signal: Peer comparison resonates
**Evidence:**
- LBGolfer (female golfer): Found peer-based benchmarking "significantly more useful" than Tour-based SG tools
- Multiple users engaged with their results and compared against their handicap bracket
- The core value prop -- "compare against your peers, not Tour pros" -- is landing
**Implication:** The peer benchmark positioning is our strongest differentiator. Double down on handicap bracket accuracy and expand bracket coverage rather than adding Tour-based features.

---

### Underserved audience: Female golfers find it more useful than Tour-based SG
**Evidence:**
- LBGolfer: "As a female golfer seems your model may even be more useful than conventional SG, as the course rating/slope account for women's hitting distances. SG using male Tour data is less useful to me as it doesn't distinguish between skill and men's longer hitting distances."
- Key insight: course rating/slope already normalizes for distance, making peer-based SG naturally gender-inclusive
**Implication:** Female golfers and high-handicap players are an underserved segment for SG tools. Consider targeted outreach to women's golf communities. The "benchmark against peers" message resonates even more strongly with players further from Tour-level performance.

---

### UX validation: Tool is quick and easy to use
**Evidence:**
- Jiinker: "Nice work OP. If and when it warms up in Michigan enough to play i will be sure to use it."
- LBGolfer: "Nice interface, easy to use."
- BOYLANATOR: Engaged multiple times; despite methodology concerns, concluded "So what you have is good."
- No complaints about input complexity or confusing UI
**Implication:** The "scorecard + 5 minutes" positioning is validated. Protect input simplicity as new features (like approach distances) are added -- make new inputs optional, not required.

---

### Validation signal: Putting and ATG stats are the primary draw for some users
**Evidence:**
- BOYLANATOR: "The putting and around the green stats are interesting to me so I would use it for that."
- Even users who question OTT methodology still find value in the categories that don't depend on distance
**Implication:** Putting and ATG are the most trustworthy categories in a stats-based approach (least affected by the OTT distance gap). Consider emphasizing these in marketing and results display. These categories may retain users while OTT methodology is improved.

---

### Validation signal: Input friction resistance confirms simplicity moat
**Evidence:**
- BOYLANATOR: "That's a whole extra layer of detail and I probably wouldn't want to enter estimated distances for every shot."
- Candymanshook's counter-proposal (approach distance via laser) was specifically designed to minimize friction
**Implication:** Users who identify the methodology limitation still prefer simplicity over precision. Any distance feature MUST be optional and low-friction. The "good enough with a scorecard" positioning resonates even among sophisticated users.
