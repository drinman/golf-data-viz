# Golf Data Viz Research Package

This folder captures two rounds of growth-focused research completed on
2026-03-17. Round 1 (files 01-06) built the evidence base and initial
proposal. Round 2 (files 07-10) stress-tested those assumptions with
adversarial counter-evidence, identified blind spots, surfaced new patterns,
and produced a revised v2 proposal.

The package is grounded in the app as shipped today, the March 10-16, 2026
analytics snapshot, and current external sources across golf, coaching, and
adjacent consumer products.

## Baseline

- The app already ships a meaningful surface area: landing page, sample data,
  strokes gained benchmarker, score-first sharing, AI narrative, round history,
  saved-round sharing, and premium lesson prep.
- The immediate problem is not feature absence. It is lack of a compounding
  loop: the app is generating interest, but not enough sharing, search
  discovery, or measured return behavior.
- The working thesis throughout this package is that Golf Data Viz should grow
  by becoming the public, score-first post-round artifact for amateur golfers,
  not by becoming a broader private analytics dashboard.

## Documents

- [01-current-state-funnel-audit.md](./01-current-state-funnel-audit.md)
  audits the shipped product, the funnel, and the instrumentation. It answers
  what the current analytics prove, what they do not prove, and where the
  product is leaking before compounding can happen.
- [02-golf-landscape.md](./02-golf-landscape.md)
  maps the direct golf market: Arccos, Shot Scope, Clippd, TheGrint,
  18Birdies, Golfmetrics, DECADE, and scorecard-adjacent products. It focuses
  on what each product really optimizes for.
- [03-coach-workflows.md](./03-coach-workflows.md)
  looks at how players and coaches actually exchange information, which artifacts
  are useful before a lesson, and why coach-shareable outputs are a stronger
  wedge than building a coach platform early.
- [04-adjacent-patterns.md](./04-adjacent-patterns.md)
  mines products like Strava, Oura, WHOOP, Chess.com, Spotify, and Hevy for
  reusable patterns in public artifacts, AI interpretation, recap loops, and
  social mechanics.
- [05-distribution-opportunities.md](./05-distribution-opportunities.md)
  focuses on discovery: benchmark-intent SEO, Reddit and group-chat behavior,
  creator and instructor formats, and public-page loops that can compound from
  a direct-heavy early base.
- [06-opinionated-growth-proposal.md](./06-opinionated-growth-proposal.md)
  is the Round 1 point of view. It turns the evidence into a recommended
  direction, a 90-day roadmap, explicit build/defer/stop calls, and kill
  criteria for the biggest bets.

### Round 2: Challenge Research (adversarial stress-test)

- [07-challenge-growth-mechanics.md](./07-challenge-growth-mechanics.md)
  challenges seven core growth assumptions with external data. Covers
  K-factor reality (0.05-0.15 not 0.3), Spotify Wrapped 2024 archetype
  backlash, OmniCalculator SEO vs sharing evidence, Reddit self-promotion
  limitations, Wordle sharing frequency collapse, monetization timing, and
  peer comparison validation.
- [08-challenge-product-blind-spots.md](./08-challenge-product-blind-spots.md)
  investigates eight strategic blind spots. Covers the Clippd + Garmin
  competitive threat, retention math for 20-30 rounds/year usage, vanity
  handicap privacy concerns, the multi-tool strategy (OmniCalculator
  playbook), AI commodity threat to SG analysis, unproven group chat
  virality assumptions, golf startup failure patterns, and international
  market opportunity.
- [09-challenge-new-patterns.md](./09-challenge-new-patterns.md)
  surfaces overlooked implementations and patterns from adjacent products.
  Covers the Round Receipt format (Receiptify), embeddable widgets (mortgage
  calculator playbook), tiered referral rewards (Morning Brew), PWA install
  retention gains, Untappd-style achievement badges, 16Personalities
  archetype naming, newsletter-as-product, course leaderboards (Strava
  Segments), and Foursome Challenges (Peloton Teams).
- [10-revised-proposal-v2.md](./10-revised-proposal-v2.md)
  is the final synthesized v2 proposal incorporating all Round 2 changes.
  Six assumptions changed, eight held up. Includes revised four-phase
  roadmap, 17-item prioritized implementation plan, updated success metrics,
  and kill criteria for every major bet.
